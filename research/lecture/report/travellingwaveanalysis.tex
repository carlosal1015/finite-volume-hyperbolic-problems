\section{Travelling wave analysis}
\label{sec:traveling}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Motivated by laboratory experiments \cite{khoshnevis2014combustion, gargar2015recovery, gargar2014recovery, zheng2017numerical, anderson2022analysis, blackwell1959factors}, it is common to search for mathematical solutions to combustion models in the form of traveling waves \cite{ozbag2018traveling, da1999traveling, ghazaryan2013gasless, gordon2006travelling, ghazaryan2011stability}.
Traveling wave solutions can be characterized as solutions invariant with respect to translation in space \cite{volpert1994traveling}.
They are solutions of the underlying partial differential equation that preserve their shape while propagating with a certain velocity \cite{ghazaryan2022introduction}.

\begin{definition}
	\label{def:traveling}
	The solution $(\hat\theta,\hat S_y,\hat S_\text{o})(\xi)=(\theta,S_y,S_\text{o})(x,t)$, depending on the traveling variable $\xi=x-ct$ with constant $c$, of \eqref{eq:14}-\eqref{eq:16} is called a traveling wave solution if it satisfies
	\begin{equation}
		\label{eq:limits}
		\lim_{\xi\to-\infty} (\hat\theta,\hat S_y,\hat S_\text{o}) = (\theta^L,S_y^L,S_\text{o}^L)
		\quad \text{and} \quad
		\lim_{\xi\to\infty} (\hat\theta,\hat S_y,\hat S_\text{o}) = (\theta^R,S_y^R,S_\text{o}^R),
	\end{equation}
	where the limits coincide with the Riemann problem conditions defined in \eqref{eq:Riemann_boundary}.
\end{definition}

Mathematically, to prove the existence of the traveling wave solution in the sense of Def. \ref{def:traveling} is equivalent to investigating the existence of the corresponding ODE solution with $\alpha$- and $\omega$-limits defined as in \eqref{eq:limits}; see \cite{guckenheimer2013nonlinear,sotomayor1979liccoes} for details.
In what follows, we drop the hats in the notation for simplicity.
When convenient, we refer to traveling waves as in Def. \ref{def:traveling} as combustion fronts as it is commonly done in combustion theory
\cite{ghazaryan2022introduction}.

\subsection{Existence and uniqueness of the traveling wave solution}

Changing the problem variables $(x,t)$ to the traveling ones $(\xi=x-ct,t)$ in \eqref{eq:14}-\eqref{eq:16} and assuming stationary solution ($\partial(\cdot)/\partial t = 0$), the original system is rewritten as follows:
\begin{equation}\label{ec4b}
	-c \displaystyle\frac{\partial \theta}{\partial \xi} + a_1 \displaystyle\frac{\partial (S_\text{o} \theta)}{\partial \xi} = b_1 S_y S_\text{o} \Phi - \textcolor{red}{\beta \theta},
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}\label{ec5b}
	-c \displaystyle\frac{\partial S_y}{\partial \xi} + a_2 \displaystyle\frac{\partial S_y}{\partial \xi} = - b_2 S_y S_\text{o} \Phi,
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}\label{ec6b}
	-c \displaystyle\frac{\partial S_\text{o}}{\partial \xi} + a_3 \displaystyle\frac{\partial S_\text{o}}{\partial \xi} = - b_3 S_y S_\text{o} \Phi.
\end{equation}

\section{Equilibrium points}

In this paper we are interested in studying the combustion from and
thus we assume that the reaction does not occur at the boundaries,
i.e., the reaction rate $\Phi$ in~\eqref{eq:34} vanish when: there
is no oxygen $(Y=0)$, which we call ``Oxygen Controlled'' case
(OC); there is no fuel $(S_{\text{o}}=0)$, which we call
``Fuel Controlled'' case (FC).
In order to approximate applications, we consider that the left
injection end of the domain is FC, and the production right end of
the domain is OC.
The OC and FC equilibria are given by~\eqref{eq:13}, then:
\begin{align*}
	\text{OC: }
	\big(S^{L}_{\text{o}},\theta^{L}\big) & =
	\left(0,0\right).                         \\
	\text{FC: }
	\big(S^{R}_{\text{o}},\theta^{R}\big) & =
	\big(S^{R}_{\text{o}},0\big).
\end{align*}

\begin{itemize}

	\item If $c<a_{3}$ and $c<a_{1}S^{R}_{\text{o}}$, then $\lambda^{1}_{\text{FC}}>0$ and $\lambda^{2}_{\text{FC}}<0$. (saddle point)
